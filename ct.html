Lung Cancer Prediction with Integrated Computer Vision and Statistical Modeling

This project focused on enhancing early detection of lung cancer by integrating medical image analysis with clinical risk factor modeling. Using over 500 CT lung scans and accompanying patient reports, I developed a hybrid framework combining computer vision (CNN models) and statistical regression analysis to improve prediction accuracy of lung cancer incidence based on both imaging features and patient clinical data.

Project Objective

  • Improve early detection of lung cancer by integrating CT imaging data with clinical risk factors such as smoking history, BMI, and comorbidities.
  • Enhance model feature interpretability by incorporating both computer vision outputs and structured clinical data.
  • Support clinical decision-making with reproducible, statistically validated models.

Workflow

  • Curated 500+ de-identified chest CT scans and extracted imaging features using convolutional neural networks (CNNs) modeled after ResNet and VNet architectures for nodule detection and classification.
  • Conducted preprocessing of CT images, including normalization, segmentation of lung nodules, and patch extraction for 3D CNN input.
  • Applied 3D-VNet segmentation to isolate pulmonary nodules and 3D-ResNet models for malignancy classification based on size, shape, and texture.
  • Integrated structured clinical data (smoking history, BMI, comorbidities) with imaging predictions using multivariate regression models.
  • Conducted statistical analysis in R to quantify relationships between risk factors and cancer incidence, informing feature selection for the machine learning pipeline.
  • Evaluated model performance using ROC-AUC, sensitivity, specificity, and predictive values.

Results and Impact

  • Achieved classification accuracy exceeding 95% on internal test sets for lung nodule classification.
  • Integrated clinical risk factor analysis improved feature interpretability and clinical relevance of imaging-based predictions.
  • Demonstrated potential for improving early screening protocols and risk stratification, especially in underrepresented patient groups.

Technical Stack

  • Computer Vision: Python (PyTorch, TensorFlow, Keras), 3D-VNet, 3D-ResNet models
  • Statistical Modeling: R (GLM, regression analysis, exploratory data analysis)
  • Data Processing: NumPy, pandas, SciPy
  • Medical Imaging: DICOM, ITK, image preprocessing pipelines
  • Evaluation Metrics: ROC-AUC, sensitivity, specificity, PPV, NPV

Key Takeaways

  • Integrated computer vision models with clinical data provide superior predictive accuracy over standalone models.
  • Addressing data imbalance and diversity is critical for equitable performance across patient populations.
  • This hybrid approach has significant potential for translational healthcare applications and reducing screening disparities.
